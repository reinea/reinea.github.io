MY WORK

I took my first computer science course in high school, where I learned the basics of Java. After discovering my interest in programming, I began experimenting with HTML/CSS on my personal blog and later on some Blogspot themes. It wasn't until college that I pieced together the puzzle and realized that I loved the technicalities of computer science and the design aspect of HTML. I decided to pursue Human Centered Design and Engineering as a result.

Below are some personal and academic projects that I have undertaken in the past few years. Each one has a particular audience in mind, focused on how they will interact and respond to the project.

PLANT APP

Interaction Design Concept, Fall 2016

As part of my HCDE 210 course, we studied interaction design through citizen science. I had to create an app that enabled everyday people to gather large amounts of data for scientists while reaping rewards and maintaining incentive. How do we design an app with the audience in mind?

    • 'CAKE POTATO' THEME

    Blog Theme Design & Build, Winter 2016

    In 2015, I decided to combine my love for writing and taking pictures in a photo blog called 'Cake Potato'. For the beginning of its third year since the launch, it needed a refresh in appearance, so I designed and built a theme that felt more personal and reflected the things I posted about.
